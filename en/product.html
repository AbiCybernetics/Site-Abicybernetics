Mobility

Walking is human

Walking is an essential human act. We rarely think about it — it is part of who we are. With every step, we feel the ground, move through the world, meet people, and gather experiences. Walking means freedom, independence, and dignity.

For millions of people with paraplegia or severe motor impairments, walking is a distant dream. Being confined to a wheelchair is not only a loss of mobility — it is also a major health burden. Prolonged sitting impacts circulation, cardiovascular health, muscles, and even mental wellbeing. For healthy people we say, “sitting is the new smoking.” For someone with a motor disability, prolonged sitting can become a real danger, accelerating complications and reducing both life expectancy and quality of life. That is why exoskeleton technology is not just robotics — it is a promise of life.

Built-in intelligence

How the exoskeleton supports walking

Four high-precision actuators at the hips and knees deliver the torque needed for standing and smooth walking. Support crutches provide extra balance and control for safe assisted gait.

Sensors monitor posture and intent in real time and adapt every step. Millisecond-level synchronized control prevents rigidity and delivers a more natural gait.

* Note: device in development; not certified as a medical device.

Modular platform

Integrated sensors

The device is equipped with multiple sensors that capture user feedback and adapt accordingly. The gait profile is highly adjustable to match your needs.

Ergonomics

Premium materials and ergonomics

Every exoskeleton component is designed for fast setup — no tools, no tangled cables, and no compromises in daily comfort.

  • High-grade aluminum frame — medical/aerospace-grade alloy with an excellent strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Robust yet lightweight — durable, while still light enough for everyday use.
  • Ergonomic padding — positioned at contact points for maximum comfort during long sessions.
  • High build quality — supports everyday use, safety, and reliability.

Your mobility

Our mission is your mobility

At AbiCybernetics, we believe technology must be more than hardware and software. It should be the bridge between physical limitations and the dream of walking again.

That is why we are building this platform with passion, attention to detail, and a clear vision: to make exoskeletons a normal part of everyday life.

Short term: strength assistance for industrial work, sports recovery, and augmentative exoskeletons for healthy users.

Long term: adaptive AI that learns each user's gait and brain-computer interfaces (BCI) that connect intent directly to movement. We imagine a world where assistive technology is not a compromise, but an extension of humanity.
